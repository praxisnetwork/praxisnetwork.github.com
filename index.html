Each program exists within a particular ecosystem of disciplinary expectations, institutional needs, available resources, leadership styles, and specific challenges.

About

This informal network was developed with support from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation as part of a 2012-2013 Scholarly Communication Institute (SCI) project on rethinking graduate education. Its goal is to highlight emerging literacies necessary to humanities scholarship in the digital age, and to offer model program that expand career opportunities for graduate and undergraduate students. Related SCI work includes a study of alt-ac career preparation as well as a series of meetings convened in partnership with centerNet and CHCI, the Consortium of Humanities Centers and Institutes.
